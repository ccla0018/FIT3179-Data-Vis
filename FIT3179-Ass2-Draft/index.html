World Freedom: 2010 - 2020

Freedom Index around the World


Accross 2010 - 2020, the World Human and Economic Freedom Index is a comprehensive and influential measure that assesses the degree of freedom enjoyed by individuals and businesses in countries around the world. Countries with high scores on the World Human and Economic Freedom Index are

often characterised by strong protections for individual rights, minimal government interference in economic activities, and vibrant free markets. Conversely, nations with lower scores may face challenges related to corruption, excessive regulations, and restrictions on personal freedoms.


Economic Freedom


Foundations of Human Freedom: Key Contributing Factors



A handful of crucial factors stand as the foundation in assessing a nation's economic well-being and vitality.

1
Legal Measures

Legal measures profoundly shape economic freedom by establishing transparent regulations that protect property rights, contracts, and intellectual property. A strong legal framework fosters investor confidence and business integrity, laying the groundwork for a thriving economy.


2
Open Markets / Trade

Open markets and global trade drive economic growth by promoting healthy competition and enabling the exchange of goods and services. Countries benefit from comparative advantages, expansion of market reach, and enhanced innovation through the facilitation of free trade agreements.

The data depicted in the graph indicates that Africa's performance in both factors appear to be relatively weaker compared to Europe and the Asia Pacific region. This highlights the need for potential enhancements in Africa's legal and trade measures to foster a more conducive environment for economic activities and growth.





3
Government Money Measure

Government spending and investment significantly impact economic freedom. Excessive government spending can lead to budget deficits and inflation, while strategic spending on infrastructure and human development can foster growth and productivity. Similarly, well-directed government investment in education and innovation can drive economic opportunities, while misdirected investments may hinder economic dynamism and freedom.

1
Expression

Freedom of expression empowers individuals to voice opinions, dissent, and engage in artistry without fear of retribution. This fundamental right fosters an inclusive environment, valuing diverse perspectives and enabling a vibrant intellectual landscape.
